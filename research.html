Enhancing Lung Cancer Screening by Analysing Clinical Symptoms Using Machine Learning

This research focuses on leveraging Artificial Intelligence (AI) to enhance early detection of lung cancer‚Äîone of the world‚Äôs most fatal diseases. Traditional imaging methods like LDCT are effective but costly and often inaccessible in low-resource settings. My study employs structured clinical data and multiple machine learning algorithms to develop a cost-effective diagnostic model.

Using the Kaggle Lung Cancer dataset, preprocessing techniques such as data imputation, normalization, and outlier removal were applied. Feature selection was performed using Recursive Feature Elimination (RFE) to identify the most impactful predictors. Models like Random Forest, XGBoost, CNN, and SVM were developed and compared, with Random Forest achieving the highest accuracy of 99%.

The results demonstrate how AI can efficiently classify normal and abnormal lung conditions, supporting faster and more affordable diagnosis. This research underscores the potential of interpretable machine learning models in revolutionizing early cancer detection and healthcare accessibility.

Published in: IEEE (2025)

Authors: Sonam Khattar, B. Vivek Gowd, Tushar Verma

Presented at: International Conference for Women in Innovation, Technology & Entrepreneurship 2025
